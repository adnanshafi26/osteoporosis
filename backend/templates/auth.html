Understanding Osteoporosis

  • Osteoporosis is a bone disease that makes bones weak and fragile.
  • The condition occurs when bone density decreases faster than bone formation.
  • It commonly affects older adults and women after menopause.
  • Common fracture areas include hip, spine, wrist and knee joints.
  • Early diagnosis helps prevent severe fractures.
  • Bones lose calcium and essential minerals, making them porous and weak.
  • It is often called a “silent disease” because symptoms appear in later stages.
  • Common early signs include back pain, loss of height, and stooped posture.
  • Low intake of calcium and vitamin D increases the risk of osteoporosis.
  • Lack of physical activity leads to weaker bones over time.
  • Smoking and excessive alcohol consumption can accelerate bone loss.
  • Family history can increase the chances of developing osteoporosis.
  • It can be diagnosed using a Bone Mineral Density (BMD) test like DEXA scan.
  • Regular weight-bearing exercises help improve bone strength.
  • A balanced diet with dairy products, leafy vegetables, and nuts supports bone health.
  • Medications can help slow down bone loss and reduce fracture risk.
  • Fall prevention is important to avoid fractures in affected individuals.
  • Men can also develop osteoporosis, though it is more common in women.
  • Maintaining a healthy lifestyle helps in managing the condition effectively.
  • Early detection and treatment reduce the risk of severe complications.
